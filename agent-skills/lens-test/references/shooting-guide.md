# Shooting Guide

Use this when the user needs a full reshoot protocol.

- Target: flat, evenly textured, fronto-parallel surface such as a brick wall, newspaper spread, or
  detailed poster. All four corners must land on texture.
- Distance: fill the frame with around 10% margin while keeping all corners on the target.
- Alignment: keep the camera axis perpendicular to the surface. Use a level or aligned verticals;
  keystone can fake decentring.
- Focus: focus once in the centre, then do not refocus through the aperture series.
- Apertures: shoot f/4, f/5.6, f/8, and f/11. Use two frames per stop when possible.
- Field-curvature disambiguator: add one wide-open frame focused on a corner when corner softness
  may be curvature rather than decentring.
- Light and stability: use even flat light, base ISO, tripod or timer when possible, and avoid
  corrected exports.
